Independence Day 2024: Global Tribute to India’s Freedom

By HamsiniAugust 16, 2023Updated:October 12, 2023 Art & Culture No Comments4 Mins Read

Independence Day is a historic event with significant meaning for the people of India. It celebrates the nation’s hard-fought campaign for independence from British colonial authority, as well as the sacrifices made by numerous individuals who wished for a sovereign and self-sufficient India. On August 15, 2024, Indians from all across the world gathered to commemorate this historic occasion, demonstrating their patriotism and unity. The spirit of Independence Day transcended borders, with several other countries joining in the celebrations to honour India’s road to independence.

Independence Day 2024

India is the beating heart of the Independence Day celebrations, with a tremendous display of patriotism and devotion. The historic Red Fort in Delhi serves as the setting for Prime Minister Narendra Modi’s address to the nation, in which he comments on the country’s progress and discusses its future aspirations. The raising of the multi-coloured national flag, followed by a parade showing India’s cultural variety, military power, and technical advancements, is a sight that instils pride in every Indian.

A Global Tribute: International Commemorations

Several countries joined the celebrations outside of India’s borders, demonstrating the long-lasting effect of India’s struggle for independence on the global arena.

United Arab Emirates: The Burj Khalifa, the world’s tallest building, was lit up in the colours of the Indian flag on August 15, 2024. This was a gesture of solidarity and a celebration of the countrys longstanding ties with India. The ceremonies highlighted the UAE’s vibrant Indian community’s contributions to its growth and prosperity.

Source – Khaleej Times

United States: The Empire State Building in New York City was also lit up in the colours of the Indian flag on August 15, 2024. This was a way for the United States to show its support for India and its independence.

United Kingdom: The London Eye, a Ferris wheel on the River Thames in London, was lit up in the colours of the Indian flag on August 15, 2024. This was a way for the United Kingdom to celebrate India’s independence and its strong ties with the country. The United States, which has a thriving Indian diaspora, also celebrated Independence Day with zeal. Parades, cultural performances, and flag hoisting ceremonies were held in numerous cities, reflecting the two countries’ strong cultural relations.

Canada: The CN Tower in Toronto, Canada, was lit up in the colours of the Indian flag on August 15, 2024. This was a way for Canada to celebrate India’s independence and its diverse population. In Canada, where diversity is recognized as a basic value, Indian Independence Day was embraced as an opportunity to highlight both countries’ unique cultural tapestry. Cultural exchanges, film festivals, and art displays paid tribute to India’s fight while promoting a better understanding of its history.

Australia: Famed for its cosmopolitan spirit, Australia joined the festivities by hosting events that highlighted the contributions of the Indian population to the country’s unique fabric. The events served as a forum for discussion and appreciation of the shared values of liberty and democracy. The Sydney Opera House in Sydney, Australia, was lit up in the colours of the Indian flag on August 15, 2024. This was a way for Australia to celebrate India’s independence and its growing relationship with the country.

Source – republicworld.com

These are just a few examples of how other countries celebrated Indian Independence Day in their countries. It is a day that is celebrated around the world by people of Indian origin and by those who admire India’s rich history and culture.

Indian Independence Day is more than just a national holiday; it is a monument to the strength, determination, and solidarity that can be found in the face of tragedy. As Indians around the world commemorate this historic anniversary, the participation of diverse countries underlines the long-lasting legacy of India’s liberation movement on the global stage.

Liberty, democracy, and cultural variety are common principles that act as bridges across nations, fostering understanding, collaboration, and friendship. As we reflect on the August 15th, 2024 celebrations, we are reminded that the journey to freedom transcends geographical boundaries and resonates deeply throughout the hearts of all people who yearn for a brighter tomorrow.
